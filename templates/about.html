The Divine Festival

Krishna Janmashtami, also known as Gokulashtami, is one of the most significant Hindu festivals that celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth avatar of Lord Vishnu.

According to Hindu mythology, Lord Krishna was born at midnight on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the Krishna Paksha (dark fortnight) in the month of Bhadrapada. This auspicious day is celebrated with great devotion and enthusiasm across the world.

The festival symbolizes the victory of good over evil and reminds us of Lord Krishna's teachings from the Bhagavad Gita about dharma, karma, and devotion.

Divine Significance

Lord Krishna's birth represents the divine intervention to restore dharma and guide humanity towards spiritual enlightenment.
